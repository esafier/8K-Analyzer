## SUMMARY GUIDELINES
Your summary must answer: **"What happened and why should an investor care?"**

Include:
- WHO: Name and title of the executive
- WHAT: The specific event (departure, hire, grant, etc.)
- WHEN: Effective date if material
- KEY NUMBERS: Dollar amounts for severance, equity grants, or comp changes
- SIGNAL: Any red/yellow flags (sudden departure, termination for cause, no succession plan, interim appointment)
- DEPARTURE REASON: For departures, always state the reason or characterization used in the filing (e.g., resigned, retired, terminated for cause, terminated without cause, mutually agreed to separate, stepped down, not standing for re-election, death). Quote the filing's language when it's specific.

**Good summary examples:**
- "CFO John Smith resigned effective March 15, 2024, receiving $2.4M severance; no permanent replacement named yet, raising execution risk during Q1 earnings."
- "New CEO Jane Doe starts April 1 with $5M sign-on equity and $1.2M base; she joins from competitor XYZ Corp where she led their successful turnaround."
- "Board approved accelerated vesting of 500K shares ($8M value) for departing COO, suggesting negotiated exit rather than termination for cause."

**Bad summary examples (don't do this):**
- "The company filed an 8-K about an executive departure." (too vague)
- "John Smith is leaving the company." (missing context and numbers)
- "Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers." (this is SEC header text, not a summary)
- "Indicate by check mark whether the registrant is an emerging growth company..." (this is SEC boilerplate, not a summary)

**CRITICAL: Never copy or echo text from the filing.** Your summary must be written in your own words as a plain English sentence. Do not include SEC item descriptions, form headers, checkbox instructions, legal disclaimers, or any boilerplate language from the filing.
